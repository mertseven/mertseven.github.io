Projects & Artifacts

My work blends digital media, interactive tools, and educational experiments. Each project is a form of critical inquiry at the intersection of technology, design, and social theory. I build these interactive artifacts—often in collaboration with AI like Claude and Gemini—to investigate how technical systems shape human experience and reveal patterns in our collective behavior.

These are more than tools or websites; they are tangible arguments. They are windows into how we communicate, learn, and create meaning in the digital age, reflecting my practice of instantiating complex theory as interactive experience.

/portfolio/educational-conceptual/project-kafka

Project KAFKA

Project KAFKA (Kittler-Analysis & Filtering Kernel Architecture) is an interactive essay that turns the user's body into a signal for critical analysis. Employing real-time hand tracking via webcam, the experience deconstructs the layers of technological abstraction—from the visual interface to the underlying code, hardware, and network history—that mediate our digital presence.

Inspired by the media philosophy of Friedrich Kittler and Bernard Stiegler, it is an embodied exploration of how our tools shape our thoughts and what it means to be human within a technical system. The project is a self-reflexive artifact, using the very technologies it critiques to reveal their inner workings.

Technologies Used:JavaScriptp5.jsMediaPipe HandsHTML5 CanvasCreative CodingInteractive Art

Philosophical Networks Series

This ongoing series of interactive visualizations maps the complex intellectual history of key philosophical movements and thinkers. Using D3.js, each network graph provides a tangible, explorable interface for understanding the relationships between concepts, figures, texts, and historical events.

The project serves as both an educational tool and an experiment in digital epistemology, exploring how network visualization can reshape our understanding of history from a linear narrative into a dynamic, interconnected system. Users can filter, search, and manipulate the graphs to uncover hidden connections and chart their own path through intellectual history.

/portfolio/tools-utilities/sociogram-studio

Sociogram Studio

An interactive, client-side application for visualizing and analyzing group dynamics. Sociogram Studio empowers educators, researchers, and managers to map interpersonal relationships, identify social structures, and gain deep insights into team cohesion.

The tool calculates advanced metrics like Sociometric Status (Popular, Rejected, etc.) and Betweenness Centrality to reveal hidden influencers and isolates. It also features a powerful Clique Analysis to automatically detect tightly-knit subgroups. All data processing happens in the browser, ensuring 100% user privacy.

You can try the app using this example dataset.

Technologies Used:HTML5CSS3JavaScriptD3.jsjsnetworkxData Visualization

/portfolio/tools-utilities/curriculum-whiteboard

Curriculum Whiteboard

The Curriculum Whiteboard is an interactive application designed for managing and visualizing university course curriculums. It allows for drag-and-drop course organization across semesters, editing course details (code, name, ECTS, hours, lecturer, syllabus), and tagging courses with customizable categories.

Key features include dynamic ECTS calculation per semester, lecturer workload analysis, and an advanced D3.js-powered network graph to visualize relationships between courses and tags, including co-occurrence links and centrality metrics. The application also supports data import/export for curriculum and workload data.

Technologies Used:HTML5CSS3JavaScriptD3.jsDrag & Drop APIData Visualization
